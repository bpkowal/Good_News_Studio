Care Ethics Response:
Care ethics prioritizes relationships, emotional resonance, and concrete human contexts. In this case, the ethical question is complex and sensitive. While abstract impartiality may suggest that lying is morally justifiable to alleviate suffering, care ethics challenges this view.

Firstly, care ethics emphasizes the importance of interdependence and relational closeness. Lying to a loved one can damage the trust and intimacy that underpin these relationships, leading to long-term harm. Moreover, care ethics highlights the importance of empathy and moral attention to the specific people involved. Lying to a loved one can demonstrate a lack of empathy and moral attention, prioritizing the ethical principle over the well-being of the individual.
Secondly, care ethics acknowledges the limitations of caring. While it is noble to want to alleviate suffering, care ethics recognizes that one cannot care for everyone. In this case, caring for the loved one may involve difficult choices, such as being honest about their condition while also providing emotional support.
Finally, care ethics encourages a nuanced and contextual approach to ethical decision-making. While abstract principles may suggest that lying is morally justifiable, care ethics recognizes that the specific context and relationships involved must be taken into account.
