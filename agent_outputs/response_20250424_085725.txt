Deontological Response:
The categorical imperative is the fundamental principle of morality that guides our decision-making. According to Kant, we should act only according to maxims that we can will as universal laws. In this case, we must determine whether we can will the action of going on vacation while leaving the store unattended.
Maxim: I will go on vacation, but ensure that the store is in good hands while I am away.
Test: Can this be a universal law? No, it cannot be a universal law because it is not applicable to all individuals in the same situation. The maxim does not provide a universal rule that can be applied in all cases.
Impermissible action.
